Memorizing

A comprehensive, collated list, of tips to memorize material, from all over the internet

Text



Disclaimer: Reading is not studying. Simply reading and re-reading texts or notes is not actively engaging in the material. It is simply re-reading your notes. Only ‘doing’ the readings for class is not studying. It is simply doing the reading for class. Re-reading leads to quick forgetting - learningcenter


Past papers

Do the sample problems and explain how you got from the question to the answer. Re-do the problems that give you trouble - learningcenter

Look for patterns. Study the marking schedule. If you need past papers, try nobraintoosmall

Make a song

Since singing requires oral, aural and physical engagement, more neural pathways form as one sings or chants than in simply saying memory work over and over - justclassical

Especially effective with movie/novel study quotes



Linking ideas

Think about where you've heard the concept before - on the news, on YouTube or TV, or in a game - inspirationeducation

Mentally link the concept to something you already find interesting.

Mindmap/cheatsheet

For English, note down some quotes on your cheat sheet. For maths, note down some equations. Anything you think is essential - inspirationeducation

Some exams let you take notes in. Sadly, NCEA isn't one of them. But it's a great way to revise!



Become a teacher

It is okay (even encouraged) to do this with notes in hand. Eventually you'll be able to teach it without your notes - learningcenter

When you can explain it out loud, without notes, you know you've got it!

Memory place

The memory place technique involves imagining a location in your mind, and placing memorable imagery inside that associate with what you want to remember. THen to remember the information, you simply walk through your place in your mind - aidanhelfant
