Memorizing

A comprehensive, collated list, of tips to memorize material, from all over the internet

Text



Disclaimer: Reading is not studying. Simply reading and re-reading texts or notes is not actively engaging in the material. It is simply re-reading your notes. Only ‘doing’ the readings for class is not studying. It is simply doing the reading for class. Re-reading leads to quick forgetting - learningcenter

If you're having issues with thinking you understand the material, but you forget stuff. You need to read this! It's all about the illusion of learning. Which I don't have time to go into more detail about here.

Past papers

Do the sample problems and explain how you got from the question to the answer. Re-do the problems that give you trouble. Learning the material in this way actively engages your brain and will significantly improve your memory - learningcenter

Look for patterns. Study the marking schedule. If you need past papers, try nobraintoosmall


Linking ideas

For example, when learning about DNA, you could think about where you've heard the word DNA before - on the news, on YouTube or TV, or maybe in a video game - inspirationeducation

Relate the concept to your life, where you hear it before, other learning, a game you like, metaphors, or analougies.


Mindmap/cheatsheet

For English, note down some quotes on your cheat sheet. For Maths, note down some of the equations you need to remember. Anything you think is essential – put it on the summary sheet. - inspirationeducation

Some exmas let you take a handwritten A4 piece of paper into the room. Sadly, NCEA is not one of them. But create one anyway. They're a great way to revise!


Become a teacher

It is okay (even encouraged) to do this with your notes in your hands. At first you may need to rely on your notes to explain the material, but eventually you’ll be able to teach it without your notes - source

This applies to memorization too! When you can explain it out loud, without notes, you know you've got it.


The memory place

The memory palace technique involves imagining a location in your mind, like the candy store in town, and placing memorable imagery inside that associate with what you want to remember. Than to remember the information, you simply walk through your palace in your mind - aidanhelfat

Just need to memorize a list of quotes or vocab? That sucks. But this technique almost makes it fun. And you'll recall stuff much longer.
