Motivation

A comprehensive, collated list, of motivation techniques from all over the internet

Rate Urgency

I like to force myself to get it over and done with first thing in my study session, so I don't have to worry about it anymore. - studytime

Write a list of the three most urgent things you need to do. Do the hardest first. Don't procrastinate. You'll feel much better.

Minimising distractions

I remind myself that I'll actually enjoy my time on social media or messing around doing other stuff more if I do it at a time I won't feel guilty about it. - studytime

Lock your phone in the car, powered off. Put a piece of paper next to you. Whenever you get distracted, write it on the sheet. Keep going.

Switch it up

This is one for really desperate times, not to be used all year round. - oxfordroyale

Get up at 3am. There are no distractions then, you'll be fresh, and can feel smug that you're up working before everyone else.

Something positive

Boost your mood by adding a little fun to something you're not motivated to do - verywellmind

Create a study space. This could be anywhere. But not somewhere you mentally associate with doom scrolling. Create a study playlist. Burn a scented candle. Bring snacks or friends. Reward yourself.

Push through

Many people think we need to feel motivated enough before doing something. However, psychological research has shown the converse, and it is the action that leads to motivation, which then leads to more action! - ucsa

Even if you don't feel motivated, just try. Push through the first painful study sprint. Get into the zone. To help, try the 2 techniques below.

Fake it

Act as if you feel motivated: ask yourself, “What would I be doing right now if I felt motivated?” Consider what you’d be wearing, how you’d be thinking, and what actions you’d be taking, and do that - verywellmind

Just start pretending. You've got nothing to lose.

10 minute rule

Once you get started with a task, it's usually much easier to keep going. - verywellmind

Give yourself permission to quit. After 10 minutes you're giving up. Just do those ten minutes. If you don't get in the zone, try getting a snack, or exercising.

Exercise!

It's been proven a hundred times over: regular exercise boosts concentration. - oxfordroyale

Get up, play some music, and dance for 15 minutes. Do some starjumps. Run around the garden 10 times. Get the blood flowing to your brain, then come back to the task.

Fuel your brain

When our motivation is low for study we can feel both stressed and drained. So boosting your energy usually starts with caring for your body and your brain - insightcenter

Very self explanatory.
