What is studying?

Long story short, studying is when you sit down with the intention to prepare for your exams. The intention is to make sure you're confident with what you learned in class, and make sure you're confident you're going to be able to deliver on the day of the exams. You want to study so that you know when you walk into the room and have three hours you'll be able to recall facts, paragraph structure, mathematical methods and physics techniques, and be able to put it all together to get the mark you want at the end of the day.

However, it's harder than it sounds. There are a lot of things that can go wrong. For example, how do you know what you don't know? What if you're just confused about everything? What if you know what you don't understand, but you don't know where to start in terms of figuring it out? Or maybe you're not in that boat at all. Maybe it's less than a week before you're English exam and no matter how well you know you're quotes and your themes, you don't feel confident about going into the room and writing under pressure?

Whatever your issue, I can help, but there are a lot of study techniques on this website, so to direct you to the ones that are going to be most helpful for you, select which of these best represents your biggest studying roadblock:


Don't know what to work on
Need to break the problem down
