The Pomodoro Technique

Enter: the Pomodoro Technique. Choosing one task and focusing on it for 25 minute intervals, taking a 5 minute break and repeating - studytime.
  • Each "pomodoro" is a 25 sprint of work where you only focus on one task
  • After each sprint you take a 5-minute break
  • After 4 in a row you take a 15-30 minute break
  • Repeat until your work is done
  • This will improve your focus and wellbeing.


Interleaving Practice

Interleaving practice is the perfect opposite to learning styles, and that's why we love it... It's all about jumping between different study methods and different topics - studytime.
  • Chuck on a 15 minute timer
  • Focus on one thing, such as brainstorming novel themes, or solving merit level algebra questions
  • Take a break
  • Start another 15 minute timer
  • Work on another subject, or another standard or topic within that subject
  • This keeps your brain engaged.
