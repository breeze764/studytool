The SQ3R Method

Ever tried to tackle a textbook chapter on a confusing topic, and gotten so overwhelmed you couldn’t take a single thing in? The SQ3R method might be just what you need. - sourcestudytime

Survey. Read the textbook/teacher's notes/example calculation. Question. Write down everything you find confusing. Re-read. Read it over again, and try to answer your questions. Recite. After each chunk of text/major step, try to explain in your own words what just happened. Revise. Write a summary of everything you learned.


The Feynman Technique

For something a bit more lowkey, but equally effective, give the Feynman technique a go. It relies on just four key steps - studytime

Step one. Study. However you like. Two. Teach it back to someone else. A mate. The wall. Your dog. Three. Study again, but this time focus on the parts you couldn't explain. Four, summarise. Drawings. Graphs. Mind maps.


Your own words

when taking notes by hand, it’s often impossible to write everything down; students have to actively pay attention to the incoming information and process it—prioritize it, consolidate it and try to relate it to things they’ve learned before - scientificamerican

Get out a workbook. Break down the ideas. Write down what you do understand. Proccess it. Try to put the pieces together.


Additional material

Videos, podcasts, and livestreams can each be a valuable way to convey information, if they exist. Experiment with different media and find what works for you - hubspot

If you can't understand the textbook or your teacher's explanation, maybe it's time to stop re-reading it.


Update your notes

Read the text again. See if you can add to your notes now that you have had some time to digest what you have read - learningessentials

Updating your notes is a great way to find holes in your old thinking and find areas to learn.


Leitner system

The Leitner System is one of the best study techniques based on flashcards - universitysa

Make your flashcards. Find three or four boxes. All the flashcards start in box one. If you learn a card move if up a box. If you fail a card move it down a box. Focus your time on the lowest box, so you aren't re-learning the easy stuff.
