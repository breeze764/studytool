Motivation

A comprehensive, collated list, of motivation techniques from all over the internet

Rate urgency

It's easy to get fooled into being thinking you're being productive productive by creating to-do lists, in depth study plans, motivational quotes, study-space cleaning rosters, highlighter-colour-code key terms sheets and so on - studytime

Write a list of the three most urgent things you need to get done this study session. Just focus on them.


Fuel your brain

When our motivation is low for study we can feel both stressed and drained. So boosting your energy usually starts with caring for your body and your brain - insightcenter

When you're stressed about studying, try getting a snack or doing some exercise first.


Fake it till you make it

Many people think we need to feel motivated enough before doing something. However, psychological research has shown the converse, and it is the action that leads to motivation, which then leads to more action! - ucsa

Even if you don't feel motivated, just try. Push through the first painful twenty minutes, and hopefully you'll get into the zone.


10 minute rule

Once you get started with a task, it's usually much easier to keep going. - verywellmind

If you really aren't feeling energized for even your first study sprint, give yourself permission to quit after ten minutes. Yes, really. Start a ten minute time. You know you can do just 10 minutes. This first ten minutes may get you in the zone. And if you not, take it as a sign you need a break. Try getting some exercise, or fueling your brain. Then come back to it.


Positive self talk

After failing a test, students spent more time studying when they spoke to themselves kindly. - verywellmind

Tell yourself you are capable of tackling the task, and that you can overcome obstacles. When you think you might fail, argue all the reasons you are going to succeed.


Exercise!

It's been proven a hundred times over: regular exercise boosts concentration. - oxfordroyale

Get up, play some music, and dance for 15 minutes. Do some starjumps. Run around the garden 10 times. Get the blood flowing to your brain, then come back to the task.


Act as if you're motivated

• Act as if you feel motivated: So ask yourself, “What would I be doing right now if I felt motivated?” Consider what you’d be wearing, how you’d be thinking, and what actions you’d be taking. Then, do these things, and see if your motivation level increases - verywellmind

Just start pretending you're motivated. Announce to the world "I am so excited to do __" and get started. You never know.


Switch it up

This is one for really desperate times, ont to be used all year round. - oxfordroyale

Get up at 3am. There are no distractions then, you'll be fresh, and can feel smug that you're up working before everyone else.


Minimising distractions

I remind myself that I'll actually enjoy my time on social media or messing around doing other stuff more if I do it at a time I won't feel guilty about it. - studytime

Put your phone away when you get home. Like, lock it in your car, powered off.


The distraction sheet

The distraction sheet has helped me no end. If you've got a brain that loves to flit from thing to thing, it'll help you too. - studytime

Have a piece of paper beside while you try to focus, and write down anything you suddenly remember you need to do instead of getting up, distracted.


Something positive

Boost your mood by adding a little fun to something you're not motivated to do - verywellmind

Burn a scented candle. Create a study playlist. Bring snacks or friends.


Reward yourself

Literally very self explanatory.

I recommend chocolate.


Study space

It's not just about how you study. Where you study is equally important - vic

This could be anywhere. But it shouldn't be the place you mentally associate with doom scrolling.


Reconnect with your why

Imagine your life in your future after you have completed your study - ucsa

Think about the scholarship money, or how happy your parents will be when you get excellence.


Hardest thing first

I like to force myself to get it over and done with first thing in my study session, so I don’t have to worry about it any more - studytime

Think about how great you'll feel!
